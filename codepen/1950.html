I thought I was the only one, feeling like this …
Impostor syndrome is a psychological pattern in which an individual doubts their skills, talents or accomplishments and has a persistent internalized fear of being exposed as a “fraud”.

Thus starts Wikipedia’s entry on the Imposter Syndrome. And even though my rational self can process this information, and knows my work is most likely “good enough” and that I'm not a fraud — my irrational self still struggles.

At the Fronteers Conference in 2017, Jessica Rose spoke about this — and for me, that was a real eye-opener. Apparently, a lot of developers feel this way. And although I can’t really do anything about it (believe me, I’ve tried!), just knowing there are many people out there with the same struggles, is reassuring.
